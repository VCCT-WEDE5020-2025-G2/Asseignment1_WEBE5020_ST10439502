A Place for Possibilities

Hope Haven of Northeast Georgia, Inc. is a private nonprofit corporation whose primary purpose is to provide services for individuals with developmental disabilities who reside in the Athens-Clarke and surrounding counties.

Mission &
Principles

Our mission, vision, and core values guide everything we do.

1.

Mission: Hope Haven’s mission is to prepare, empower and connect adults with developmental disabilities to strengthen our community.

2.

Vision: We envision a world where adults with developmental disabilities will be able to fully integrate into the community independently.

3.

People & Purpose-Driven: We embrace diversity by recognizing individual talents. We celebrate abilities by supporting a vibrant life centered on expressiveness and choice.

4.

Innovation & Inspiration: We promote growth by opening the door to endless possibilities. We encourage and assist individuals in self-advocating, practicing unique skills, and motivating one another to grow and flourish.

5.

Collaboration & Inclusion: We utilize a team approach that includes the individual, family, friends, and community to advocate for inclusive activities supporting all aspects of life.

6.

Dignity & Respect: We recognize the worth and potential of each individual we serve, protecting their dignity and respecting their rights, opinions, and choices.

Program Quality & How We Work

Individuals who receive services from Hope Haven should expect to gain opportunities for increased community participation. Each individual is involved in developing their own Individualized Service Plan, identifying goals specific to their strengths, abilities, preferences, and desired outcomes. Services available are outlined in our brochure, handbooks, and website. Our Program Specialist can provide consultation regarding services, wait times, and organizational certifications such as CARF accreditation and DCH licensure.

Organization

Hope Haven is privately governed by its own Board of Directors, comprised of respected community leaders. The Board holds an annual fundraising event to help support operating costs, fund capital improvements, and provide equipment and vehicles not funded by the state.
